## Implications:

There are about 9 different categories that are usually looked at during projects, those being:

#### Global:
* Government/Laws
* Big Systems (Such as Education, Healthcare, etc)
* Environment
#### Social:
* Individual
* Relationships
* Culture
#### Economic:
* Jobs
* Finances
* Infrastructures

For this program, I'll focus on the following categories: Big Systems, Individual, Relationships, Jobs & Infrastructures.

### PROS:
* **Big Systems:** *Education facilities such as schools could use the app to encourage Spanish learning for students while also letting them have fun.*
* **Individual:** *Language learning greatly improves a person's memory, and this program helps them unlock aspects of the world that's been hard to reach due to language barriers, specifically Spanish.* 
* **Relationships:** *A person with a significant other/friend/family member who speaks primarily Spanish could easily find volcabulary to engage in a proper conversation with them.* 
* **Jobs:** *Due to this program's education based nature, it's highly probable to help people pursue careers that request people who are bilingual/multilingual.*
* **Infrastructures:** *With the program's focus on Spanish learning, it helps encourage conversation with foreign contractors and architects, leading to more uniquely designed structures.*
### CONS:
* **Big Systems:** *The shorter levels for this program may leave students uninterested in learning Spanish through gameplay.*
* **Individual:** *This program does not cover Spanish accents, so the player may be left confused upon seeing a written word with its correct accent mark.*
* **Relationships:** *There is no speaking aspect of the program, so a person may have a hard time speaking to a foreign loved one.*
* **Jobs:** *Jobs might not consider language learning when looking for candidates, leaving someone stranded if they relied solely on their language skills.*
* **Infrastructures:** *This program doesn't cover certain categories of Spanish (such as fruits, colors, etc), so signs on buildings in Spanish may become difficult to translate with the knowledge from this program.*
