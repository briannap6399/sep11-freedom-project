# Entry 6: End of Course Reflection
##### Brianna Peralta on June 1st, 2026 (06/01/26) 
## Main Information: 

And.. Done. I know that sounds weird to include for the beginning of a Blog, but I'm deliberately saying this now because this is the last blog I'll be writing for SEP11, woohoo! 😃 With the ***[product](https://albertog3410.github.io/sep11-freedom-project/tool/kaboom/kaboom-prompt.html)*** of all SEP11 Students now completed, students had to continue with their Freedom Project by putting together a ***[presentation](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1m79h2I8hXE-Ybe_TbApceDgVLC0R6EAXaKzxhsH9Rv0/edit?usp=sharing)*** that not only showed off the final project (also known as the product) but also showed a good chunk of the coding process, potential struggles you or your partner had along the way and your final lessons (or takeaways) from the entire project. Once the presentations were done, which included a few days dedicated to sharing them to your respective class, we then had to work on an 'Elevator Pitch' (essentially a really short introduction that only takes about a minute) that grabbed the attention of not just fellow students on the day of the Expo but also a panel of judges who were grading all of the projects. Clearly the last few weeks had been chaotic, but hey, we made it! The Expos for all Computer Science courses are done and boy do I have a lot I need to say. Of course I'll go into the details in the later sections but long story short all preparations were, at least for me, way more hectic than it was for last year's Expo.
