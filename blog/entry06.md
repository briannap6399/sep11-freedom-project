# Entry 6: End of Course Reflection
##### Brianna Peralta on June 1st, 2026 (06/01/26) 
## Main Information: 

And.. Done. I know that sounds weird to include for the beginning of a Blog, but I'm deliberately saying this now because this is the last blog I'll be writing for SEP11, woohoo! 😃 With the ***[product](https://albertog3410.github.io/sep11-freedom-project/tool/kaboom/kaboom-prompt.html)*** of all SEP11 Students now completed, students had to continue with their Freedom Project by putting together a ***[presentation](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1m79h2I8hXE-Ybe_TbApceDgVLC0R6EAXaKzxhsH9Rv0/edit?usp=sharing)*** that not only showed off the final project (also known as the product) but also showed a good chunk of the coding process, potential struggles you or your partner had along the way and your final lessons (or takeaways) from the entire project. Once the presentations were done, which included a few days dedicated to sharing them to your respective class, we then had to work on an 'Elevator Pitch' (essentially a really short introduction that only takes about a minute) that grabbed the attention of not just fellow students on the day of the Expo but also a panel of judges who were grading all of the projects. Clearly the last few weeks had been chaotic, but hey, we made it! The Expos for all Computer Science courses are done and boy do I have a lot I need to say. Of course I'll go into the details in the later sections but long story short all preparations were, at least for me, way more hectic than it was for last year's Expo. 

## My Reflections: 

Let's start back at the Presentation. Before we could even think of putting together the final slides, all students first had to ***[plan](https://github.com/albertog3410/sep11-freedom-project/blob/main/prep/presentation.md)*** out how they'd break the whole thing down. This not just included like a general format but if possible it also included phrases or anything in particular you potentially wanted to memorize. Since I was a part of a partnership for the Freedom Project, my partner and I did a lot of the general planning together, but I remember developing a humorous idea for the opening sentence of the presentation. As previously mentioned in past blogs, my partner and I created a platformer that tried to help enhance a person's vocabulary under the Spanish Language. It's an educational game, and I wanted to emphasize its significance by deliberately challenging people and their current knowledge of Spanish. The opening phrase, which we agreed would be said by me since not only was I the one who proposed it but I also was the stronger of us 2 in Spanish, was an entire mini section that talked about the Spanish Regents (the big exam this project tried to prepare for). The plan was to leave people confused while also saying the section correctly so Alberto can then take over and begin to further explain what's going on while hinting at what the project ended up being.

With that being said, the only problem with this part was actually ensuring I had the entire thing memorized and perfected. Other than that, the rest of the planning was simple, and for the most part, so was the presentation. Admittedly I did get a little tripped up with our plans for it because the presentation itself didn't exactly go how it did when we were in the midst of planning it. Our project, which is more of an error on our end, didn't format properly onto a bigger screen so it was way too stretched for it to be playable and at points I felt myself freezing up internally to the point I caught myself stuttering a good amount. Even with the minor errors however, the presentation came and went with positive feedback and I'm deeply grateful for that. However, the real problem started happening during the week of the actual Expo: I was forced to be absent from almost every school day that week besides, ironically enough, the Expo day itself, and during my absence, the Elevator Pitch was created and practiced. I didn't have much time to really digest what it consisted of and I had to really focus on Alberto so I can continue what he's trying to say without many errors. It took a **LOT** of confidence and observations, but even that was done smoothly, so I suppose all of my worrying was for nothing? Not exactly honestly since that nervousness did lead into me trying to be the best I could possibly be, so that's a win in my book!

## The Engineering Design Process:

I think it's safe to say that my partner and I are officially on the last step of the Engineering Design Process, that specifically being ***Step 8 - Communicate the Results***. Taking the feedback from our peers, we took our project and began to improve it, mainly by adding more content and more intense challenges as mentioned in Blog #5. In addition, as we were putting the presentation together, we received all sorts of feedback, ranging from needing to enhance eye contact to shortening the Spanish Hook. Admittedly I didn't shorten the hook as I already memorized it all and was frequently practicing everything as flawlessly as I could. However, I did fix my eye contact, not distributing it properly among everyone in the room but making sure I was consistently looking rather than avoiding eye contact at all. It's an improvement that'll not only become more enhanced over time but it also paves the way for similar levels of improvement, which did culminate into the Expo, as my partner and I surprisingly had a decent amount of visitors to our station (besides the judges of course). Admittedly I do wish I had the additional time to bug fix the game since I realized a lot of that ended up taking up a lot of our elevator pitch time, and it might've cost us the win as a result. But regardless, I still love this project, and I'm so glad that others, especially those taking Spanish courses and or trying to learn the language, share that same sentiment. 

## Final Skill Check-In & Wrap-Up: 

Last but certainly not least, how have the skills festered? As a quick reminder, they were both ***Embracing Failure*** and ***Organization***. I don't know why but throughout my time writing these blogs, ***Embracing Failure*** would frequently slip my mind.. Then again, that could be classified as me embracing that failure, hah! But either way I definitely improved on that due to the amount of chaos that had occurred for me during these last few weeks. It was all very hectic and at some points I genuinely thought I'd ruin the project my partner and I worked so hard on with my own errors: However my partner was there for me every step of the way, helping me stay updated with the project and sometimes taking the lead such as with the Elevator Pitch. Alberto, if you somehow read this whole thing, thank you for all of the help.. And sorry for all of my writing, but you already know I like to write a lot so am I really sorry?.. Somewhat. Alright with that being said, what about ***Organization?*** It certainly had better days. I was a mess these weeks, as I've said.. Several times now. However if I had to give myself credit, I feel like I was really organized with the planning phase of our presentation. I was the one who did most of the planning, mainly due to my partner not having much time of his own to do a good chunk of his sections. I suppose him having my back during the Elevator Pitch was like me having his back with the planning now that I think about it. Whether anyone really sees it how I see it or just thinks what I said made absolutely no sense, at least we can agree on the fact that this year was surprisingly a lot more fun than I thought it would: I was always told I'd dread the 11th Grade and hate it with a passion, yet this year was probably the most fun I've had during my time in HSTAT? It's weird, but I personally call this year the ***'Year of Opportunities'*** due to everything that's happened to me, from this Freedom Project but even down to my musical. Thank you for helping me develop this ***'Year of Opportunities'***, and who knows, maybe you'll catch me in APCSA! Only time will truly tell.

-Bri
